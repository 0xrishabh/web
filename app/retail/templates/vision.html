Blockchain evolving over time

This is our best bet as to how the space is going to evolve. By building a map of where we think its going, we can map out how Gitcoin can help

{% endblocktrans %}
Now
  • Sharding / POS in research stage
  • Bear Market
  • Gitcoin is doing some Revenue Testing (Tokenization, Secret Project, CodeFund, Grants, Bounties)
1 Year out
  • Sharding / POS goes live.
  • Stablecoins are an accepted store of value.
  • Sideways Market
  • Some ICOs will launch / many will fail to get market traction / most ICOs will fail altogether
  • Consensys hits 1k people
  • Gitcoin has figured out a scalable Revenue model. Starting to scale.
3 Years out
  • Ethereum hits 1 billion tx / day
  • The flippening happens
  • Blockchain disrupts the dollar for store of wealth for nation states. To subvert sanctions at first and weaken the influence of he US dollar for countries like Russia
  • Identity on blockchain because a very big deal
  • Megabull Market
  • Consensys is tokenized
  • Gitcoin is a $20mm / year business.
5 Years out
  • It is as easy for a software developer to find work as it is for an Uber driver to find a ride 10 years ago.
  • Gitcoin is a $50mm / year business.
10 Years out
  • Whether or not ETH ‘wins’, xyz cryptocurrency will be utilized for a non-trivial amount of total currency transactions. (i.e. at least 10% of total M1 money supply)

Open Questions about the Future

We're not sure about these parts of the blockchain-ized world.

{% endblocktrans %}

Blockchain

Will blockchain be in everyone's wallet (Browser), or will it be hidden in the back? (Linux)

What will Identity on web3 look like? Will there be a dfb (decentralized facebook)

What web3 wallet will everyone use?

What will maturation/consolidation in the blockchain space look like?

OSS

What is a scalable funding model for developers (esp OSS) in a blockchain world?

Can blockchain solve The Software Paradox?

Is Github’s centralization a problem?

The Future Of Work

Will software developers find work as easily as Uber drivers do today?

In that scenario, how will ancillary services (Retirement, Insurance, Health Care) build around a ‘future of work’ scenario? I’d imagine, more so, than just making it economically feasible to work 4-5 OSS projects as a full time job, many people wouldn’t want to give up benefits that they accrue in their day-to-day lives.

Are these government based, “employer” based, or none of the above?

Got input that can inform our vision?
